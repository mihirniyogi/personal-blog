There's something about musical movies that makes you feel so good. A friend introduced me recently to this genre and I feel like I've been missing out! So I've started watching...

I realise these include both Disney classic films (animated or live action) as well as the more contemporary ones. The common thread seems to be that the characters break out into song when they have something they wish to express; a feeling; a thought; strong emotions; a strong will. 

### my past

Musical movies, movies upon which musicals are based and musicals themselves are all included in the following list. There's not really a clear separation, and besides, they all make me feel a similar, amazing way. I plan to update the following continually :- 

- Hairspray (2007) | Musical Movie
- La La Land (2016) | Musical Movie
- Legally Blonde (2001) | Movie (but there's a musical based on this)
- The Hunchback of Notre Dame | Musical (caught in 2026; live in Melbourne)

### why?

Why do I like these? Not only do they make me feel, but it's like the possibility of expressing yourself in all these different ways gets unlocked. Singing is a form of expression, which is why I want to go for singing lessons eventually. Dancing is a form of expression, which is why I attended all those dance classes across Melbourne, with the same friend actually. It's giving me _access_ to the emotions I'm feeling. The bridge between my mindscape and my feelings is being built. The highs become higher and conversely, the lows are lower. 

Coming to the point, these musical/movies push forward this journey of mine. The lyrics, the music, the dancing have intricacies and nuances of the emotions being expressed. Even if they're more happy-go-lucky, like the ones in Hairspray, or tragic, like those in Notre Dame. That's the primary reason, anyway. 

Also, they can be pretty idealistic! The world is _usually_ simpler, a little less gray, dull, more full of colour and life. I think that's just fun to watch. 

And the songs. OH-EM-GEE. Those from musicals/musical movies are usually absolute bangers. You can play them over and over again. In the shower. While cleaning. While travelling. Notre Dame had _banging_ tracks.
