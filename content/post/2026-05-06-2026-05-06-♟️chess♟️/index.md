For the past few weeks, I've been avidly eating, playing and sleeping chess. Every lunch break I get I put on a video from [GothamChess](https://www.youtube.com/@GothamChess) (the goat of chess teachers/entertainers). Right when I wake up, I play a rapid game (the 15 minute variant with 10 second increments). All this to say I've improved from an 800 rating to just 860 LOL.

The people I've met in chess club are also brilliant. Everyone's humble and always making self-deprecating jokes about blundering their pieces in one move (my kind of humour). There's free pizza every meet. Every Wednesday morning I get to both socialise and get some practice in. All this for a one-time $5 fee??

### the game

The game of chess is invigorating. It may look like there's only a few moves to start with but there's opening theory, decision-making, long-term strategic planning, logical flows, permutations and a whole such world hidden within the movement of the pieces. When my plans proved successful and I won two games against a higher-rated player than me, the feeling was unmatched. Not because I won, but because I felt myself accessing higher-level skills than the previous weeks, and the strategies I used to win actually worked.

### the community

Here on exchange I'm getting chances to try new things and get better at them, because we have to start somewhere, right? Be bad before being good. More than that I'm finding that I'm enjoying the _community_ of chess. I look around in the middle of a game and see some teaching others crucial openings or explaining their mistakes. It's this really wholesome, learning-filled, judgement-free zone; a _community_. 

As I try out new things, either on campus or off, I am constantly amazed at the community. There's something to be said about these third-places where people go to relax, have fun, socialise. I don't think we have many such places back home in Singapore...

I have people expecting to see me there next week as well! Which means I have a week to practice and continue learning this amazing game. Yay!
