Today felt incredibly relaxing. 

I started the day with an Iced Matcha Latte 🍵 from Little Rogue in the city (top 5 matchas I've tasted for sure). Before my appointment at 11.20 at the spectacle shop, I had some free time so I sat at the open tables at QV. The wind was _strong_ and almost blew my drink away. I just felt so relaxed, what with the wind blowing through my hair; sitting like a boss without a care in the world. I might as well have been on a beach sipping a (Virgin) Piña Colada. What a marvellous city. 

### a spectacle of an experience

I purchased new glasses - my old ones bent from being punched in the face during boxing class. The sales person was really sweet and we bonded over Singapore! Apparently she's been a few times and kept mentioning Lau Pa Sat as a highlight. After the exchange, we exchanged IGs and offered to bring her around the lesser-known gems in Singapore if she were ever to visit again. Again, the feeling of _marvel_ - would anyone in Singapore do this? Exchange socials with a customer?

### grass

I subsequently caught lunch at Central and decided right there and then: let's sit on some grass. I've been between the initial admin tasks of working remotely in a startup (friend of a friend is the founder) albeit part-time. I alighted at Flinders, walked along the River until I hit Alexandra Park, and sat under the shade of a tree on the soft, welcoming grass. 

I played a chess game online to "relax" before commencing on the startup work. I even lay down using my bag as a makeshift pillow (a very Melbournian thing to do I think) and ... scrolled IG. But there's a difference between scrolling IG on your bed and in the big open spaces that are Melbourne's parks.

### finale

Finally, I travelled to UniMelb and did some work here too within the Old Engineering Building. I write this post as I take my break. I can feel my energy levels trickling down just a tad, as is natural. My brain's auto-generating plans for the rest of today: buy a few groceries, go home and watch MasterChef Australia on the TV.

Having such good days where I'm surrounded by nature and also the cool city I think replenishes something I call a "hope level". It's this reassurance that everything is going to be OK. It's not a big-picture thing, but just, living life better. Not to say that I don't have deadlines or assignments or work; I absolutely do. The key difference here is that when you're in and around nice people and nice places, it gives you a sense of confidence in facing challenges. Life becomes as if on easy mode; smooth-sailing; relaxing; cool; _chill_. I am less scared of my immediate future.

I might start the following "X of the Day" thing. I don't post daily, so it's really "X of the short phase of my life I'm in right now". To be improved.

Cheers until next time,

\~M
