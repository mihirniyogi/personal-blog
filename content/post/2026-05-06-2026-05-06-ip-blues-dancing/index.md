A friend and I have attempted the many, many dances of Melbourne! Despite having two left feet and being incredibly awkward with my body, I've picked up on the basic steps of not just Salsa, but Blues, Swing and Line Dancing!

### salsa

Introduced by another friend of mine who stays here long term, the free beginner Salsa class in the CBD by [The Salsa Foundation](https://www.thesalsafoundation.com.au/) is something I think everyone should go for once. After the beginner class, an official course just happened to be starting right after and they let us attend this first iteration for free! Difficult as it was, I did like the vibes - _smooth, sexy, swaying_. 

{{ < youtube tGGUDnq4P1I >}}

Obviously, our moves were not as smooth as Jai (the founder/teacher) and the partner dancing with him in the video. The teachers were great and the class was so friendly to beginners and all ages. Fantastic experience overall. I think this was when I first thought to myself that dance is more accessible than I thought, and anyone could at least learn the basic moves.

### blues and swing

I _love_ blues music. For me, this was the peak of the dance journey. We took a train all the way to Fairfield and walked in the rain but it was worth it. Everyone was grooving to the beat and this felt more natural and improvisable than salsa. However, this was still a lead-follow system so there was a little more pressure on the leads (usually males but it doesn't matter) to decide the moves and maintain the pace. 

{{< instagram DX8lUMkJLlJ >}}

I liked the slower pace though - it allows me to slow down and intentionally choose what the next step is going to be. Shob and Joel were great instructors and paid attention to everyone. The vibes here were _slow, groovy, relaxed_. This was peak. 

![Peak meme](peakmeme.jpg "20%:")

{{< instagram DYVjqVhBe5y >}}

Swing dancing was a lot more fast paced and spazzing with energy. Fun, but tiring (and a little expensive). At the end of the class, they had a little farewell dance where everyone took turns dancing with the person that was leaving (we had no context of course). Imagine living here and building your community circle. Imagine coming back every week and meeting the same people. So much so that they throw you a _farewell dance_ when you do leave.

### line dancing

This has been on my bucket list for a while. Everyone knows what line dancing is! We went to this cowboy, Western-style pub, Moon Dog Wild West in Footscray. We were taught three different routines (two of them had each iteration end with a 90-degree turn, so you would turn 360 eventually). The music was a blast, notably [Tulsa Time by Don Williams](https://open.spotify.com/track/0cThXovxrvU68JuzYUXhJT?si=41450b6e17114125), and the energy of the place was electric. We rode on the famous Mechanical Bull too! So much fun. 

### dance exchange

All this wasn't for nothing though; my friend and I taught a 3rd friend - let's call her D. D had chosen to join the full Salsa 1.5 course, so she taught us her moves and we taught her ours! We danced midday, in the middle of Carlton Gardens, on the grass. I kind of forgot about my worries and was just in the moment, enjoying the struggle of learning new dance steps and finding the humour in that.

I believe the friend and D both went to a final Salsa party; "La Fiesta" to top it all off. I declined as I needed a bit of a rest day. Overall, what a fantastic experience. This is easily in my Top 10 Melbourne experiences - one of the highlights of my exchange for sure.
