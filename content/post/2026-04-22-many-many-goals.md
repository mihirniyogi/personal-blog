I have goals, so many things I want to achieve in a short span of time. I want to 

- get better at chess, learn openings and variations to improve my rating
- read up and get more into Greek Mythology
- try out different dances; salsa, blues, swing, line dancing
- explore specialty coffees in Melbourne
- explore parks to chill in in Melbourne
- plan trips around Melbourne
- plan trips around Australia (Cairns, Gold coast, Tasmania...)
- go for comedy shows by my favourite artists
- go for my favourite musicals
- get into shape by going to the gym and learning the different routines, muscle groups etc.  
- catch up on my math lectures specifically (I'm caught up on everything else)
- level up my style with clothes, accessories
- most importantly, become a better version of myself

Dare I say, juggling all this is a little stressful? That is 13 bullet points. I am only human and can't (and shouldn't) give equal focus or even cover everything all in one day, week or month. Writing these down at least helps alleviate the pressure from the vague, floating, built-up list I had in my head. Instead of _pressure_, why not I see these as opportunities? I wouldn't normally get to do these back home. Thus, every day, I get to choose what I want to do here. And it would be perfectly OK not to have completed any one of these goals. I am human and my interests are ever-changing, fluid and dynamic like the tides. 

Dare I say, the real magic of Melbourne is that I can do whatever I feel like, whatever makes me feel good. The whole point of the exchange experience is to have the space to try out new stuff in my own time and decide whether I like it or not. 

> **I am only human**
