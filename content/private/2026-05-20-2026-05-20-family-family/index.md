After a call with mom, I'm not feeling so good. During the call, I gave her some emotional advice on how to communicate and interact with my sister. It felt great and we were talking about dealing with emotions and processing them.

I believe the reason I'm feeling a little down is because the "dealing back and forth" with sis and mom has brought up some traumatic feelings. Maybe it was that whole incident last year. It's not even that I remember the incident in great detail - it's that I was put in the position of my past self, my _old_ self. 

I have a new personality here (?) which means how I act, speak and portray myself is different here than it is back home. The interactions and people are different. I'm no longer in the context of family. I'm not "stuck" in my old roles (bad as that may sound). 

I want so badly to be a different person when I get home. I envision myself like this nirvana-status guy, returning home all emotionally stabilised or whatever. Which has some truth to it - I am proud of myself for being a little more emotionally regulated and staying true and authentic to my feelings. I am able to understand, accept, connect with and process my feelings better for sure, partially due to this journal. 

Naturally, being put in that old role of back and forth has me a little out of sorts. I guess I want a perfect family or something? Where we're all positive, all of the time. But that's not the ideal to achieve. To paraphrase what my mom said in the call, the ideal family is one that communicates out feelings openly, perhaps a little diplomatically. We shouldn't be embarrassed for having feelings (we're human, after all) and neither should we judge others for having them. If person X of the family feels bad that person Y said this, I think they should process it first, and then if needed, communicate this out loud to Y. Bottling them up and not showing anything is not great. 

I think the hardest thing will be the continuation of my new self, and the juxtaposition against my old role. It's awkward, of course to be a new person in a family setting. And it's the easiest thing to revert back to "normal". I'm gonna try my damned hardest not to do that.

Am I feeling better at the end of this? Not exactly. But at least the feeling has been understood, accepted and processed. 

Signing off for now. I'll probably continue watching my lecture, do the quiz, get home and watch masterchef and heat up my leftovers for dinner. Sounds a good plan as any.
