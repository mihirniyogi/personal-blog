I have no idea why, but for the past few days I haven't been feeling particularly good. I will try to verbalise my feelings as best as I can. 

**Why i'm not feeling good** - i'm not sure yet. 

**Feelings about the feeling** - i feel guilty for not feeling good. i'm in melbourne, for god's sake. is it weird that i thought i woud always feel good here on exchange? humans are good at pattern recognition after all,  and thus far, I've been feeling great. 

Let's explore why i'm feeling suck-ish in the first place. 

Is it boredom? Is it lack of activity? My last hangout with people I know was literally 2 days ago (Monday, 20 Apr). I attended a lecture, went for boxing class, then ate dinner with a close friend. Tuesday was trying out a new coffee, getting grocery shopping done at QVM (mood was great). Weather was a little hot, so I retreated to my room, heated up leftovers for lunch. Caught up on a lecture. I think this is where I felt down again. So i decided to experiment with making black-bean quesadillas by buying the ingredients at Woolies and following a recipe. It went great, tbh, even if I was alone. I ended the day cleaning my desk and the floor, for hopes of feeling better. It did help. I postponed a call with a secondary school friend because of this Moreover, the Sunday before was jam-packed with exciting activities (caught a footy match, went salsa dancing). 

Is it lack of progress? Is it a lack of a distinct motivation? I think we're getting warmer. The assignments round had completed suddenly last week and left me with little-to-no work. 

But what I want to make absolutely clear to myself is that - its OK not to be ok. You don't _have_ to feel good all of the time. The goal here is to get better in touch with your emotions. If i'm being honest with myself, the "feeling bad about feeling bad" is stronger than the "feeling bad" itself. It matters how I react to my thoughts and feelings, not the thoughts and feelings themselves. And how I choose to react right now...is that it's perfectly ok not to be feeling ok. All I can do is continue my base routine, sneak in a few fun activities like coffee exploration or going to a park and chilling, and just live my life. Make the right choices. 

Weirdly, I'm already feeling better as I write this, which further proves that it was the secondary feelings that were worse than the primary.
