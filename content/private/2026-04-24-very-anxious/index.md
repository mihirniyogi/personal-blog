I'm suddenly feeling quite anxious. It's this weird feeling and I don't know where it's coming from. I have boxing in 30 minutes which I _should_ be looking forward to but I'm not. There's a _call_ at 10pm with a friend's startup (I think the founder wants to get to know me before allowing me to work, which is more than fair). 

Tomorrow I have the fitzroy market thing with Ben. 

I'm not sure why I'm not feeling good about the upcoming activities. They're all activities I chose, willingly. Boxing - something I've been wanting to learn for so long and I am finally getting the opportunity to do so. The startup call - it's actually a wonderful opportunity where I will get to contribute my skills and this adds great value to my portfolio. The market tomorrow - I guess that's fine actually, no biggie. 

Why aren't the former two "no biggies"? My chest feels a bit heavy as I type this. Why don't I feel _confident_ going into these? I feel like if I were confident, the activities would go by like a breeze. Also, there's no stakes! Where's the anxiety coming from??

Maybe it's that I'm not living in the present moment. As I type this, I took a look through the window, outside of the study room I'm in, and I felt ever-so-slightly better. I think watching lectures all day kind of put my mind in a frenzy. And looking back to just 30 minutes ago, I was getting fearful that I wouldn't be able to pass my Linear Models subject, due to the 80% weightage on the exam. There's no individual hurdles too, so I guess it should be fine. When the time comes, I (usually) trust myself to be able to lock in, trust my intelligence and work ethic, and just grind revision out. Usually, the grades I get are better than the worst-case-scenario my mind conjured up. 

It's going to be ok. These activities are meant to be enjoyed. No stress, no pressure, no stakes. Isn't that the point of exchange here? 

Maybe I'm just tired. And the anxiety is my body's way of demanding a mental break. Just time to myself to cook dinner, clean up, clock out early. Even that simple routine is kind of calming to think about. I feel like I'm not "free" even though I _chose_ and _planned_ my schedule. 

I think there could be some benefit from reframing my perspective here - that I _chose_ boxing. I'm choosing to go. If I didn't want to go, I simply wouldn't. But I want to go. Why? So that I get a little exercise in, catch up a little with friends, get my blood pumping, eventually lose some weight or at least maintain my fitness levels. 

As for the call, I literally created that opportunity out of thin air. I prompted the co-founders and they decided it was a good idea and decided to accept help, and even scheduled a call with me. I _get_ to contribute. I _want_ to contribute. This is how I build my portfolio, and my future. 

So even though I'm not exactly happy about me not having the ideal cool-down evening routine, I think the stuff I chose to partake in instead are just as meaningful, and more about the long-term.

It's going to be OK. It's going to be OK. Side note: meditation may be useful. Let's do this.
