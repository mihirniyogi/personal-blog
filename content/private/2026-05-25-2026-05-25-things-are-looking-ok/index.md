It's been a whirlwind of emotions. Claude says the insecurity I'm feeling isn't because of _her_ (think Helpless in Hamilton), but rather that it's just unfamiliar territory. I've never felt anything like this before, so I think my mind's in a fight-or-flight state. It's simply new. At this point, I've grappled with this feeling for so long that I don't think it's a fluke - it's genuine. 

Day by day, however, I feel myself getting better. I'm getting to a more normal state, going back to routine things. Sometimes I feel like shit, but at least it's not all the time now. I knew Melbourne was gonna be a lot of firsts, but I didn't think _this_ many. 

I'm also feeling grateful - I was just telling Ben this - for the people I've met. I don't know how others feel about this, but I think this group is awesome and we connect really well. Ben is basically my closest friend on exchange. A huge part of my growth can be attributed to these friends of mine.

Don't lean into it too much, but don't repress it either. Let it float in my head, until it slowly fades away. That's the way to get through this, I think. I'm allowed to enjoy moments, however. Moments of humour, of fun, of teasing, of joy.

### intention 

It's pretty awesome we have shared interests though (the collective 'we' now only refers to N and myself). I'm into musicals and she into musical movies. I signed up to be an assistant in NUS' musical theatre production. The intention behind it I'm trying to figure out now. The question to ask is - would I have still signed up had I not known N? 

Maybe not. But why not? Why have I signed up now? 

Upon further introspection, I think the intention is purer than I thought. I want to meet more friends who have the same shared interests. I want to explore this side of me. This is a branch in my life that's worth expanding, for sure. Plus, I told N because you know - wouldn't it be good to sign up for something with someone you know? Put bluntly, it's not to impress her or whatever (maybe a tiny, tiny part of me). But her excitement for such things is contagious; I'm remembering the love of theatre and shows and plays that I've long forgotten about or repressed or didn't care to do anything about. 

### moving forward

Moving forward, I think the way to be my most authentic self, my best self, is to simply have the purest intentions and motivations. No slimy, hidden emotional logic. Before I do or say something, maybe a thought to do a simple CI/CD check before committing to the repo. A test case to pass before it's pushed onto production. Maybe this way can reduce my anxiety of being unauthentic or being fake. Simply be true. Actually think about opinions (whether they are truly yours) before sharing them.

The trend is upwards. It's getting better. And it's going to be OK. No more self-pity, or wallowing in my misery. I have a great life. An excellent one. I'm expanding horizons and constantly learning and improving and exploring and living. If I continue to do just that, it's going to be OK.

> It's going to be OK.
