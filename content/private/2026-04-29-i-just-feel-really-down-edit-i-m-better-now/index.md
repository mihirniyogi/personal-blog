I voluntarily joined as a developer in a friend's startup (friend of the friend is the founder). For the past few days, I've been working on tickets assigned to me. It looks like there's obstacles at every step of the way. The first ticket had this massive Windows setup issue (I eventually solved it by setting up my dev env in WSL).  The second ticket is three bugs in one for some reason. And boy oh boy, are there issues. 

Firtly, the surface-level issues. The dev environment is different from the prod environment. The analytics dashboard system sucks, so it's really hard to identify the bugs from the user recordings in the first place, let alone fixing them. So at every point in this development process, I've faced problems. I guess this is normal in most first-time developer setups, but it doesn't make it any less frustrating. 

Secondly, the people-level issues. The founders are both really nice actually. But the main guy seems pretty strict about —

_And I know this is a private journal. But I haven't name-dropped them or shared intimate details about the startup because I don't want to feel like I'm in that problem-causing situation again, if that makes sense._ 

— completing the assigned tasks on time. I did say I couldn't complete the second ticket today because of school work (when really, I had gone out with a friend to Albert Park because I desperately needed a break). He replied 'no worries' and had me double confirm my new estimated completion date, which is tomorrow. He's nice, understanding even, but ... there's no buts. 

### it's me, it always was

No buts. It's actually thirdly ... me-level issues. I think the worst part of this all is that I brought it all upon myself, onto myself. If anyone were to read this I don't think that they would think these are "big" or heavy problems to be facing. But to me, this thing is the biggest thing in the world. I even cried a bit after a call with Mom, because the emotion had to release somewhere. 

I've been sacrificing my sleep, my physical health (skipped boxing), my social life (skipped chess club), my academics (skipped lectures). My daily routine took a hit. I no longer have time to slow down and smell the roses. I'm stressed. I'm anxious. I'm always checking my messages, always checking the time.

I also feel like a complete idiot (why not add this one to the mix, right?). I'm extremely insecure about my own intelligence and abilities; whether I would be able to match up to my peers; whether I could contribute to another company; whether I could code as well as the rest. 

Yes, it's a great opportunity. It's a portfolio-padder, for sure. I'm already learning an incredible amount. It would be a terrible shame if I stopped. Every step of the way, the founders look like they're planning more long-term. One says he has fun projects planned for me (he unsarcastically means it). Common sense dictates (and I think most people would agree) that I should fully pursue this opportunity. And if I were other people, I would probably say the same. 

In fact, I did. My sister says she doesn't like her admin job at the hospital at all. Repeatedly, I've suggested and recommended her to at least carry on until 3 months are over and then it would "look good" on the resume. I myself am fully guilty of this. 

But now that I've experienced it, I know better; that I'm unwilling to sacrifice my social life; my physical and mental health; my routine; my _EXCHANGE EXPERIENCE IN MELBOURNE_; for this. It's simply not worth it. I honestly don't know why I'm still debating this, being indecisive. It's 12:33am as I write this. I already know tomorrow is going to be bugs 2 and 3 from the 2nd ticket. I don't know what to do.

Ok I sent the above to chatgpt and it was suprisingly clear and logical in its response. I know what to do, I think. 

Long story short, it boils down to a lot of self-criticism and self-judgement. In reality, I'm more than capable. Any human being, however great they are, I believe would struggle to some extent in a high-friction fast-moving environment. But of the two issues I've solved, its 2/2, a 100% success rate. I'm _capable_. It's not that I _cannot_, it's that I'm _no longer willing_. 

I am feeling a little better and clear-headed now. I'll sleep over this. Let's make tomorrow a better day. Wake up a bit earlier hopefully (8am, let's say?), take care of ourselves (give adequate time to freshen up) and move. 

Cool beans.
