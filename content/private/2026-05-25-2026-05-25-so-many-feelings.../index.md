I had an incredible time last night. Angelica, Nat and I bought snacks, dinner and watched Hadestown in my Scape's cinema room. While they didn't enjoy the musical that much (which like what?? I feel like if they got into it they may have), we had so much fun after. 

Blind karaoke where I seem to have gotten many of the songs right. Nat shared her love of musical _movies_, which I wasn't into before, but I see the appeal. I'm halfway through Mamma Mia right now. In the queue are The Sound of Music and a few others she mentioned. 

Ok, who am I kidding? This is a private journal, but even admitting this to myself feels wrong. I really enjoy A and N as friends, but I caught myself looking at N a little too long. I want to say in admiration? I don't know. She's really fun to be around. As incriminating as this sounds, I'm not exactly sure it's what I (and Ben) think it is.

Ben gave stellar advice though. As with most feelings, it's ok to have this one. To repress it down completely or completely lean into it are two extremes one shouldn't be going towards. He says, and I agree, that it's important to be honest with yourself, and that there _can_ be a gap between how one feels and how one acts. A level of discrimination, per se. 

So that's the main feeling talked about, kinda. 

I feel kind of like shit because i enjoy having N as a friend, and having this feeling come in between is like an unnecessary burden. But that's the thing about accepting feelings right? To let them pass, you first have to let them in. No need to give in to everything but just open the door. I am a human being, and it's ok and natural to have _those_ kind of feelings towards another human. I'm also feeling like she's a great person to be around, and I'm also feeling she's a little judgemental. Those two - I'm not really pushing down. It's the one that I'm beating around the bush about that I'm trying to accept.
